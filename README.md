# Incident Response Portfolio
**GRC105 — Incident Management | International Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics Academy**

> A complete, end-to-end incident response programme built from the ground up — covering policy, planning, team structure, a live ransomware simulation, stakeholder communications, and post-incident improvement. All documents produced individually for a fictional healthcare technology company, TechMed Solutions.

---

## 📌 What This Portfolio Demonstrates

This repository shows the **full incident response lifecycle** — from building the framework before an incident happens, through managing a live ransomware attack, to the lessons learned and improvement plan after. This is the kind of work an IR Manager, GRC Analyst, or Security Consultant would own in a real organisation.

---

## 🗂️ Portfolio Structure

### Part 1 — The IR Framework (Theory, Week 20)

Five documents that build a complete IR programme for TechMed Solutions — a fictional healthcare fintech handling PHI and PCI data.

| Document | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| **Task 1 — Incident Response Policy** | Governing policy (Policy No. 02/Y2025) — purpose, scope, definitions, roles, classification levels, regulatory obligations |
| **Task 2 — Incident Response Plan** | Operational playbook — CSIRT activation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery and post-incident steps |
| **Task 3 — CSIRT Structure & RACI Matrix** | Org chart, role definitions and full RACI matrix for every IR phase — Core Team, Extended Team, External Team |
| **Task 4 — Incident Communication Plan** | Stakeholder register, notification timelines, communication templates, regulatory notification requirements |
| **Task 5 — KPIs & Continuous Improvement Framework** | MTTD, MTTR, MTTC, MTTRec metrics, measurement approach and programme improvement cycle |

---

### Part 2 — The Ransomware Simulation (Practical, Week 21)

A live simulation of a ransomware attack against TechMed Solutions. Five documents produced in real time, as if the incident was unfolding.

| Document | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| **1 — Decision Log** | Chronological log of every decision made during the ransomware incident — who decided what and when |
| **2 — Stakeholder Communications Portfolio** | Full set of real communications sent during the incident: CISO alert, executive briefing, regulatory notification, member notification letter, employee communications |
| **3 — Incident Action Plan** | Live action plan — evidence preservation, containment actions, eradication steps, recovery sequencing |
| **4 — Final Incident Report** | Complete post-incident report — incident overview, timeline, impact analysis, response assessment |
| **5 — Lessons Learned & Improvement Plan** | Structured lessons learned with immediate, short-term and long-term improvement actions |

---

### Part 3 — Disaster Recovery Simulation (Week 21 — FinanceFirst Bank)

A separate business continuity and disaster recovery simulation for a fictional bank, FinanceFirst, covering a data centre failure scenario.

| Document | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| **Practical Task Document** | Phase-by-phase DR execution — initial event response, recovery execution, validation |
| **After-Action Review (AAR)** | Post-simulation review — what worked, what failed, what changes are needed |

## 🏢 Scenario Context

**TechMed Solutions** (fictional) — healthcare technology company handling Protected Health Information (PHI) and payment card data (PCI). Regulatory obligations include HIPAA, PCI DSS and applicable data protection law.

**Incident simulated:** Ransomware attack — file servers encrypted, data exfiltration claimed by threat actor, ransom demand received. CSIRT activated at 6:45 AM.

**FinanceFirst Bank** (fictional) — financial institution. Scenario: data centre disaster requiring full DR activation and recovery.

## 🔑 Key Skills Demonstrated

- Writing and owning IR policy and governance documentation
- Designing CSIRT team structure with clear roles and RACI accountability
- Managing incident communications across internal, executive, regulatory and public audiences
- Applying the full NIST IR lifecycle: Prepare → Detect → Contain → Eradicate → Recover → Post-Incident

- Measuring IR programme effectiveness through defined KPIs
- Conducting structured after-action reviews and building improvement roadmaps
